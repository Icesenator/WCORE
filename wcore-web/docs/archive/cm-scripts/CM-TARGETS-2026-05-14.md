## Rejected Hook

Post tested on 2026-05-14 and rejected by owner. Do not reuse this wording:

```text
Drop a public wallet address.

I will check what WCORE can see across supported chains and share the fragmentation pattern.

Read-only only. No signatures. No approvals.
```

Follow-up if someone drops a wallet:
- Scan only public data.
- Share a short summary: number of chains with assets, top chains by value, visible dust/noise, and whether assets are concentrated or fragmented.
- Do not expose sensitive assumptions. Do not claim ownership or identity.
- If scan is incomplete, say what was visible and what was not.

Why rejected:
- Too direct for a young account.
- Feels like asking strangers for wallet addresses before trust exists.
- Better used only after a user already asks for help or shares a wallet voluntarily.

## Better Hook Direction

Use proof-based posts instead of asking for wallet addresses immediately.

Examples to test:

```text
Most crypto portfolios are not hard to track because of the balance.

They are hard to track because the context is scattered across wallets, chains, bridges and old experiments.
```

```text
The longer you stay in crypto, the more invisible your portfolio becomes.

Not because assets disappear.
Because they spread across chains you forgot you used.
```

```text
Read-only tracking is underrated.

No signatures.
No approvals.
No execution layer.

Just answering one question first: where are the assets?
```

Use the product scan offer only as a reply after someone shows intent, asks for a tracker, or volunteers a public wallet.
